Qualifying Run Defined

Students must make three (3) satisfactory runs at 30 MPH without hitting any cones.

Students will make the appropriate lane change at the given speed and exit through the center of the course no less than 2 MPH difference from entry speed.

Course Objectives

  • Learn the decisional process and how it relates to space and time judgement.
  • Learn not to rely on brakes for all maneuvers when attempting to avoid obstacles.
  • Understand the importance of vehicle placement as it relates to corner entry.
  • Understand the changes in vehicle dynamics as speeds are increased.
  • Learn that you can steer around an obstacle quicker than you can stop the vehicle.

Qualifying Run Defined

Students must make three (3) satisfactory runs.

Students will chose between look back and mirror methods before placing the vehicle in reverse and complete the course with hitting any cones with 39 seconds.

Course Objectives

  • Learn to control a vehicle while traveling backwards.
  • Understand vehicle dynamics (caster effect) in backing up.
  • Understand limitations of view while backing.
  • Understand why backing is one of the three maneuvers that police officers are more likely to become involved in a crash.
  • Understand that slower speeds are better when in reverse.

Qualifying Run Defined

Students must make three (3) satisfactory runs at 50 MPH.

Students will enter turn at minimum speed of 50 MPH.

Students will align vehicle in proper lane alignment and brake prior to entering turn for no more than one (1) second.

Student will use proper “LATE APEX” to complete turn without leaving the course or hitting any cones.

Course Objectives

  • Identify the decisional process and how it relates to space and judgement.
  • Identify how to analyze choices and execute maneuvers all at a required speed.
  • Understand that driving is a far more mental process than physical.
  • Understand the importance of vehicle placement and stopping distances.

Qualifying Run Defined

Students must make three (3) satisfactory runs at 21 seconds or less.

Student will drive in the direction given by the instructor with hitting any cones.

Use of brakes on any portion of this skills course is disqualifying.

Course Objectives

  • Learn the decisional process and hot it relates to space and time management.
  • Understand the relationship of vehicle placement to corner entry.
  • Understand the importance of proper seating and how it contributes to overall driver control.
  • Understand steering inputs and time when going around obstacles.

Qualifying Run Defined

Students must make three (3) satisfactory runs at an average speed of 28 MPH.

Student will maintain the 28 MPH speed throughout the exercise without hitting cones.

Course Objectives

  • Utilize proper seating and hand position while driving.
  • Utilize proper timing of steering inputs, or how to time your steering when driving around obstacles.
  • Develop hand and eye coordination.
  • Understand the relationship of feet per second and how it relates to timing of steering around obstacles.
  • Understand weight transfer and how a little change in speed can make a large change in how the vehicle reacts.
  • Learn proper steering skills (shuffle steering) that will be needed for more complicated maneuvers.
